Skill → Offer Mapper: The offer hiding in the skills you already have.

Pick your top three strategic-seller skills. The mapper ranks five offer types against your skill stack and shows which one is closest to a first paid customer.

Your Top 3 Skills

Toggle the three skills that describe what you do best. Most strategic sellers have 2-3 active skills and 4-5 dormant ones.

Why three. One skill is a tactic. Three combined is a category. The intersection of your top three is what makes your offer hard to copy and easy to price above market.

Pricing Architect: Four models, scored against your stage.

Hourly, package, retainer, and one-time product. Each has a different ceiling, a different fit signal, and a different trap. The architect ranks all four against your inputs and recommends the one most likely to ship a first customer inside 90 days.

Your Inputs

Pricing isn't math, but math sets the floor. Hourly caps your week. Packages cap your buyer. Retainers cap your client list. One-time products have no buyer ceiling, but the audience ceiling does the work instead.

Distribution Channel Selector: Where strategic sellers actually find their first buyers.

Six channels scored against your network size, writing comfort, and time-to-first-customer target. The selector recommends one starting channel and flags one to add after your first 90 days.

Validation Playbook: Five interviews, three betas, two pilots.

The single biggest mistake strategic sellers make on the bridge is building before validating. The playbook below is the same discovery-to-close pattern you already run on enterprise deals, applied to your own offer.

Total time
4-6 weeks
Cost to run
$0
Go / no-go signal
2 paid pilots
Why validation before building. Every hour spent building a course or community before you have two paid pilots is an hour against the wrong offer. The interviews and betas cost nothing. The course you build wrong costs you the next six months.
